A sometimes derisive term for Self-Taught Magician, though many Hege Witches will use it themselves. It references Witchcraft, which is an older, more archaic form of Magic. Depending on the tone it can be descriptive, complementary, or derisive. Hedge Witch can also mean self-taught genius as Hedge Witches tend to be incredibly intelligent, having done a whole doctorate degree by themselves.
 
Hedge Witches are looked down at by Academic Magicians, viewing them as cobbled together, and not really grasping the fundamentals, but Hedge Witches often discover because of their style of learning that Magic isn't as set and scientific as Academic Magicians like to pretend. Often Hedge Witches will know wildly different ways to perform the same spell, and can seem inscrutable by Academic Magicians who only cast using USC (University Standard Casting).
