**Along with Humans and Dwarfs Gnomes are among the founding species of the modern world. With their focus on technology and academics, Gnomish structures pervade all industry and technology. While the Dwarfs may have built society, and the humans spread it to all corners of Talahm, it was the Gnomes who dragged it from the dark ages through the illumination, or at least that is what they say about themselves. Unfortunately this aspiration has a darker side, as Gnomish Supremacy was a key philosophy in the inner circles of the Crimson Court.**
 
**Founder Species.** Gnomes are one of the three founder species of most societies, especially the Telustravaulte species of the mainland of Talahm. Because of this they often occupy a space of privilege within society as spaces and conventions are made to accommodate them. This means of course that most societies have universal gnomish suffrage, but on the other hand Gnomes can often be blind to how society treats them better than others.
 
**Age.** Gnomes reach cultural maturity at around 40 years old, and can live upwards of 450 years. Despite this, unlike Dwarfs, gnomes never lose track of the finality of life, largely living as if it will end tomorrow.
 
**The Useful Gnome.** "Handy as a Gnome" is a common aphorism around Talahm to reference the hard working culture of Gnomes, Gnomes long having a reputation for enthusiastic work at even the minorist of jobs. This comes from the fact that Gnomes for most of Talahm history, until the Industrial Revolution, occupied thankless busywork in society, which is reflective in their names. "Pencilsharpner" or "Pagestainer" isn't a random nickname, but the name of a job Gnomes created to become useful in broader society. The broader Cultural attitude of Gnomes to "be useful" is said to put an amazing amount of pressure on young Gnomes, but older more set and comfortable Gnomes usually seem ok with the convention.
 
**The Inventors of Society.** Gnomes have been long remarked on for their gadgets and knickknacks, but it wasn't until the Mago-Industrial revolution that these creation really became adopted by broader society. The Thaumaturgic Engine, The Automotion Cart (Moties), the magical assembly language of the filigree, all Gnomish inventions, and all have shaped the modern world of Talahm. For this reason the idea of inventors have pervaded Gnomish society, and their education conventions. All gnomes learn how to tinker, and some basic expressions in filigree, though proficiency varies from Gnome to Gnome.
 
**Gnomish Hospitality.** Gnomes are world famous for their hospitality, believing strongly that being or seeming unwelcome is bad luck. It is because of this that their homes are fitted for tallfolk as well as Gnomes, as it is considered rude to not be able to host guests. Gnomish houses tend to be well decorated, and bursting with their personality, as the Gnomish practice of caring for ones home or Gastlicheit, is among the central Hospitality attitudes in Gnomish culture.
 
**A Strange Perspective.** It is said that the average Gnome sees the world from a 45 degree angle, and this often leads to an odd perspective. For a long time Gnomes didn't have the concept of a Enlarge/Shrink person spell, but an Enlarge/Shrink WORLD spell, which had the exact same effect. Because of this they often act as a bridge between the stubborn and curmudgeonly Dwarfs and the ever changing and versatile humans.
 
**Work hard, Play harder.** Gnomes are as well known for their ability to party as they are their diligence in working. Famously, there are more festival days on the Gnomish Calendar than there are days, though traditionally only major festivals are observed with holiday status. There is a deep culture of drinking to celebrate business deals, and partying through the night at any opportunity. This comes with both the lively notion of Gnomish Parties being incredible, but also the grim picture of Gnomish businessmen waking up in alleyways covered in vomit. As most things there are up sides and downsides to Gnomish Party Culture.
 
### **Name Conventions.** 

Gnomes have a peculiar naming convention, as they often have two syllable first and second names, and a last name of a minor occupation, owing to the old Gnomish way of filling in minor but needed positions. Human scholars have noted that many Gnomes have names that sound like spotting four things from around a room and jamming them into a few names, and ending with a miniscule applications. While this is a stereotype, it is not inaccurate to how Gnomes are named. In addition, Gnomes often collect nicknames from family, friends, and accomplishments, and tend to notate them in official documents.
 
- **First and Second Names.** Dovetail, Briskback, Dortak, Tabelbar, Bukbag, Paintbol, Shubal, Cotfram, Naylrak, Switchmark, Clearlight, Thumbrum
- **Lastnames**. Pencilsharpener, Brushcleaner, Panduster, Gearoiler, Pictureframer, Canvaswiper, Pagestainer, Tanscraper, Candlewicker, Lamplighter  

**Religion.** Gnomes tend to follow the scriptures of the Radiant Word and worship the Scattered God, like the other founding races. While there are traces of their former Nature Worship religion, especially among Grovenvaultists, such things are ancient concerns, barely remembered but for an academic interest. Gnomes are not generally particularly religious, many are tacitly religious though a growing segment are atheists. When gnomes practice they tend to trend toward the Prismatic Sects instead of the Illuminated Church, spawning from many regional disputes in Telara amung the Gnomish concentrations. The famous Candlewicker Restoration was started when Baylik Wulbrush Candlewicker, a Tudor for the Illuminated Church wrote a scathing article of corruption practices in the church for a local journal, and thus the Prismatic Sects are more associated with Gnomes. Gnomes, when religious, tend to favor the Warmth of the Ember Flame associated with Forge and the Hearth.
   

### Gnome Traits

**Creature Type:** Humanoid  
**Size:** Small (about 3-4 feet tall)  
**Speed:** 30 feet  
As a Gnome, you have these special traits.  
**Darkvision.** You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.  
**Gnomish Cunning.** You have Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws.  
**Gnomish Education.** Gnome education is among the most focused in the world, all Gnomes in Gnomish culture are taught the fundamentals of machines granting them proficiency in Tinker (Int), in addition gnomes become proficient in one more skill.

### Gnome Subcultures

Gnomes are the smallest group by numbers of the big three in society, so they tend to gather in smaller concentrated communities, forming distinct subcultures, with practices and traditions. This has served in large part to preserve ancient beliefs and traditions within a modern context. Many cities have Gnomish neighborhoods that serve to maintain a unique gnomish identity, but it was under these circumstances that the Crimson King first got his following.  
**Grovenvaulist.** The first Gnomish civilizations can be found off the shores of Grovenvaulte, and the proud early traditions of the original Grovenvaulte natives still persist within Gnomish Culture. Most Grovenvaultist neighborhoods center around a large tree, that is often decorated with paper flags and lights, and will sit in the middle of a park. This tree used to have religious significance, but now its significance is more vague, a simple place of importance.

- Grovenvaultists tight community structure and philosophy of community care has led to a culture of learning the [Guidance](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/spell:guidance) cantrip to help others in the community, in addition they commonly [Protection from Good and Evil](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/spell:protection-from-evil-and-good) which they can cast once per day, using either Charisma, Wisdom, or Intelligence which you pick at character creation.

**Western Telarans.** The mechanical mind of the Gnomes of Western Telara are estimated by Gnomish scholars to largely be what is responsible for the Industrial Revolution of the turn of the 17th century. The developers of the first Engineering Schools, Western Telarans of Uhterhest made the first trains, engines, and developed the burgeoning field of Mechanical Magic.

- You know the [Mending](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/spell:mending) and [Prestidigitation](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/spell:prestidigitation) cantrips. In addition, you can spend 10 minutes casting Prestidigitation to create a Tiny clockwork device (AC 5,1 HP), such as a toy, fire starter, or music box. When you create the device, you determine its function by choosing one effect from Prestidigitation; the device produces that effect whenever you or another creature takes a Bonus Action to activate it with a touch. If the chosen effect has options within it, you choose one of those options for the device when you create it. For example, if you choose the spell's ignite-extinguish effect, you determine whether the device ignites or extinguishes fire; the device doesn't do both. You can have three such devices in existence at a time, and each falls apart 8 hours after its creation or when you dismantle it with a touch as a Utilize action.
