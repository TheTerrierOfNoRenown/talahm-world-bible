**Normally agrarian and botherless by nature, Halfling society until very recently has failed to affect the broader Talamh Solas, usually choosing to stick to their smaller, agricultural communities. But the worldwide urbanization has made this impossible, and turned the once carefree community into a mistrustful and perpetually scared populace. Some have chosen to urbanize, and their natural luck has suited them well in the business world and the stock exchange. Others have stuck to their small towns where their waning relevance and job options have begun to decay their cheerfulness.**   **Creature Type:** Humanoid
**Size:** Small (about 2-3 feet tall)
**Speed:**30 feet

*As a Halfling, you have these special traits.*

**Cynical.** Halflings have grown into a culture of cynicism, granting them advantage against persuasion checks and against the Charmed condition.
**Halfling Nimbleness.** You can move through the space of any creature that is a size larger than you, but you can't stop in the same space. 
**Luck.** You gain the Lucky feat.
**Naturally Stealthy.** You can take the Hide action even when you are obscured only by a creature that is at least one size larger than you.

## **Goblin**
 
**Said to be the product of some halfling tribe that violated all rules of hospitality, the Goblin have preternaturally bad luck. Houses they live in have roofs cave in, cars they drive get flats, families that welcome find themselves in ruination. Because of this Goblin have a reputation for mischief. The roof didn't suddenly cave in, it's sabotage; your car didn't get a flat, the goblin slashed the tire; your families luck didn't turn, the Goblin turned you against each other. All are stories told about these unfortunate creatures. Goblin are often shunned by society as child snatching saboteurs.**
 
**Creature Type:** Humanoid  
**Size:** Small (about 2-3 feet tall) 
**Speed:** 30 feet 
As a Halfling, you have these special traits  
**Cynical.** Halflings have grown into a culture of cynicism, granting them advantage against persuasion checks and against the Charmed condition  
**Halfling Nimbleness.** You can move through the space of any creature that is a size larger than you, but you can't stop in the same space  
**Unassuming.** Goblin are used to hiding themselves to operate in society, advantage on all stealth checks and may hide as a Bonus Action.
 
**Jynx.**  
You are naturally unlucky, which affects you and anyone around you, friend or foe. At the end of each long rest, the GM rolls a d100 on the table below to find out the effects of your bad luck. If the GM feels like the effect rolled is unlikely to happen within that day, they roll again. The GM then selects who or what is the target of your bad luck, and when the effect takes place. The effects of your bad luck can affect any creature (including you) or any object within 100 feet of you. The GM should evenly distribute the effects of your bad luck between you, your party, and NPCs (including enemies) within that 100 feet radius. Once the effect has taken place, you gain the ability to impose disadvantage on any dice roll, even those related to abilities or effects you are not aware of, provided the creature affected by the disadvantage is within 100 feet of you. Once you’ve imposed this disadvantage, the GM rolls on the table again, and so on.  
At the end of a long rest, any effects that have not yet taken place, including your ability to impose disadvantage, are discarded and the GM rolls on the table again.
