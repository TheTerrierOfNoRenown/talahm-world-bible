**Along with Dwarf and Gnomes, humans rank among the three founder species of modern civilization. Almost every major civilization is dominated by these three. Humans, owing to their short lifespan, quick reproduction, and curious disposition make up the bulk of the populace of most nations in Talahm Solas. The Dwarfs find their flexible and everchanging nature useful for industry, while the Gnomes enjoy their willingness to adopt change and explore new boundaries. Humans occupy every level of governance, every walk of life.**
 
**Founder Species.** Humans are one of the three founder species of most societies, especially the Telustravaulte species of the mainland of Talahm. Because of this they often occupy a space of privilege within society as spaces and conventions are made to accommodate them. This means of course that most societies have universal Human suffrage, but on the other hand Humans can often be blind to how society treats them better than others.  
**Versatility Comes With Unset Traits.** Humans being quick lived and incredibly versitile don't generally have a set attitude, as they are never as a group outsiders to any culture. Humans make up the bulk of every culture they live in, and thus often adopt the customs and mores of that culture. Humans from Baohu are often significantly different than humans from Uhterhest or the Vassal States, and rarely share traits in common. This versatility makes them a strength, but also leaves them far more identical to their surroundings than having unique culture to themselves.  
**Human Naming Conventions.** Humans tend to have traditional names associated with their culture. Humans from Uhterhest will often have Uhterhestrian names, James, Charles, Benedict, etc. Whereas humans from Baohu will have Baohumian names. Xing, Han, Xiang, Liue, etc. Something of note is how they do surnames, which often denotes a job (Smith, Tanner, Miller), a place (Langston, Herbershire), a landmark (Hill, Brook, Dale), or relation to another person (Jamesson, Butcherson). This comes from Medieval naming practices, where people would denote common Johns or James' with a descriptor, usually with the words "of the" or "the" between. James of the Hills, John the Butcher's Son, Charles the Tanner. Many common speaking Human cultures have dropped the "of the" though other languages often keep the equivalent.  
**Age.** Humans reach adulthood in their late teens and live less than a century.
 
**Alignment.** Humans tend toward no particular alignment. The best and the worst are found among them.
 
**Religion.** Humans tend to follow whatever religion is most popular in the area they live or are from. The majority of Humans, especially the Telustravaulte Humans are members of the Illuminated Church, though humans populate just as well the Parishes of the Prismatic Sect and the burgeoning Nimos religion.
 
**Stereotypes**. The following are stereotypes and attitudes that are common among Humans about the other species. Note: Stereotypical thought varies wildly within members of species, and expressing such outloud is considered taboo.
 
- **Dwarfs** Reliable but Boring, if you need something done, ask a Dwarf. Beyond that leave them to their rituals.
- **Gnomes.** Hardworking and Clever bastards. Give gnomes a chance and they'd fine tune the world, that's both a threat and a promise OR Bastards sold us out for their supremacy project, followed the Crimson King, wish they'd followed them to their graves.
- **Constructs.** Odd things, they are uncanny how much they want to be like us, unfortunately they constantly fail
- **Elves.** Really never seen one, read about them though, they sound so cool.
- **Tiefling.** Whenever a Tiefling Tribe comes to town, lock your doors, and stay in until they leave or they will rob you blind.
- **Halfling.** Stodgy little fuckers. Whoever told you they were salt of the earth must have had salt in the eyes.
- **Dragonborn.** Funny culture, but hardworking. First on the ship out when we need austerity.
- **Orcs.** I don't think I'm allowed to say stuff like that anymore.
- **Goliath.** Better than Orcs.
- **Aasimar.** Angelic beings, and portents of good tidings.
- **Chimera.** Creepy things, shame in how they were made. Wouldn't want one around though.
- **Gnoll.** Brutish barbarians, hear they even eat their own kind.
 
- **Size.** Humans vary widely in height and build, from barely 5 feet to well over 6 feet tall. Regardless of your position in that range, your size is Medium.
- **Speed.** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and one extra language of your choice. Humans typically learn the languages of other peoples they deal with, including obscure dialects.
