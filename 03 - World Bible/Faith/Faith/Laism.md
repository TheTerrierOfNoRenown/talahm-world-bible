In the core of all things, the great chorus sings th song of existence. Every person, place, thing, or even are but notes in the great song of the universe. But one note too often gets lost in the chorus: La, the note of connection, that which binds all other things. And it is the sacred mission of the Laist to pursue that note so that we may once again enjoy true connection with the universe.
 
Laism, founded 2500 years ago, is like many Talahm religions, native to the Draconic Conclave. Popular among dragonborn, but really taking off in Baohu, Laism holds that there is a connection, a note, that binds all things together. This note is called La, and Laist monks spend their life attempting to understand it. This concept of connection through the heavenly language that permeates all things synergizes well with many Magical teachings of the east.
 
Concepts:
 
**La**. La is the name given to the lost note that represents the connection between all things. While the note itself is not La, the word serves as a place holder until the note is found.
 
**The Great Repeat.** Laists believe that the great song of the universe has no true end, but instead at the end has a repeat, to begin the song again. Mirroring this every chord and refrain of the song in which represents the life of a person also ends in a repeat, and thus a people are reborn after death. The only way to rest from this song is through the La, the note that connects all.
