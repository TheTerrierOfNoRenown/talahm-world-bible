In what historians call the "Pencilsharpner letters" terse exchange between Sinclaire Teylis and Woolbill Teetermaze Pencilsharpner a Clerk for the Grand Emperor of the Uhterhest Empire Seymore Lioncreste XXIII, the Clerk, a line can be found: "Furthermore: the minor trade concerns of the ragabash denizens of a handful of Vassal States is far below the notice of the Grand Emperor."
 
This line was roundly mocked among the colonies, printed out, heated over, and became the subject of open letters and fiery speeches. It is considered along with the trade embargo with [[Baohu]], and several restrictive laws to keep order, one of the major events that lead to the Revolution of the Vassal States. In every letter from [[Sinclaire Teylis]] since then and all the way through his Custodianship he would sign it "The Ragabash Denizen of the Vassal States". It became so common to refer to the Revolutionary Army as the Vassal State army that it stuck and became part of the final name of the union.
