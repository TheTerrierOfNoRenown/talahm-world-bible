The Burg Fritz National Museum is a floating castle located in the center of [[Briarbrook]], that was converted into a Museum following the

Burg Fritz was the project of the the [[First Baron Fritz]] of [[Briarbrook]] upon being bestowed the land around [[Fort Cape]]. Following the redirecting of the [[Elhana’s Canal]] around modern Briarbrook, he commissioned a floating fortress: Burg Frtiz. Today it floats as a museum welcome to all comers, accessible by a set of wire lifts.

## Description

Burg Fritz floats above the Fritz Triangle. Resembling a rounded roof castle on top of a floating island, Burg Fritz has several satellites and towers flanking a large central round roof building. It is plated with copper that has patina's over the years, giving it a green gleam. Several ducts from the castle spritz water that land into a small man-made reservoir below, which filter under the city back into the canal.

## History

Founded in 25 Amethyst, DL 1412, the floating castle was commisioned by [[Baron Ezekial Fritz]] to established his dominance over his newly purchased property. Famously this put the original colony in an extreme amount of debt and it was only because of the floating nature of his castle the Baron Fritz survived the first two peasant uprisings, and was only killed in the third one when his son pushed him off the edge into the reservoir below.
